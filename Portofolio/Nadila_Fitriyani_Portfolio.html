2024 · Institut Teknologi Bandung · First Author
Spectroscopic Analysis of Planetary Nebulae IC 5217 & IC 2149
Abstract

Planetary nebulae (PNe) represent the final stage of evolution for intermediate-mass stars (1–8 M⊙), playing a crucial role in the circulation of matter within the galaxy. This study focuses on the identification of emission lines and the construction of velocity maps from the nebulae IC 5217 and IC 2149, aiming to explore their dynamic characteristics. Data were obtained from observations using the Potsdam Multi-Aperture Spectrophotometer (PMAS) with a spectral resolution of ~1500 and a spatial resolution of 1 arcsec, which were then processed using the p3d software to generate datacubes. The emission lines identified for both IC 5217 and IC 2149 include Hβ, [OIII], and Hα, with additional lines from HeI and [NII] detected for IC 2149. Velocity maps were created based on Doppler shifts observed in the emission lines, providing a detailed view of the gas motion within the nebulae.

2024 · Institut Teknologi Bandung · Co-Author
Morphological Analysis of Planetary Nebula NGC 6886
Abstract

In this paper, we report the first investigation of NGC 6886 utilizing IFU data, allowing us to build a more comprehensive morphological study based on many emission lines. NGC 6886 is a young planetary nebula featuring emission lines with varying levels of excitation. Using data from the Potsdam Multi-Aperture Spectrophotometer (PMAS), we construct an integrated spectra and identify 27 emission lines. We calculate the flux ratio of each line relative to Hβ. Lines stronger than Hβ are [OIII] 5007, [OIII] 4959, [NII] 6584, Hα 6563, [NII] 6548, and [NeIII] 3869. We also construct 16 emission line maps to examine the morphology and ionization structure of NGC 6886 and show that there are two geometrical structures within the nebula.

2023 · Himastron ITB × Bosscha Observatory
User-Friendly Astronomical Image & Data Processing Program
Abstract

P Cygni type stars exhibit unique spectral characteristics, offering insight into their mass loss and evolutionary stages. In this research we present a study on the spectrum of P Cygni (spectral type B1-2Ia-0ep, m = 4.8, α = 20h 17m 47.2s, δ = +38° 01′ 58.6″) observed on July 14, 2023, using SCT Meade LX200R telescope and LHIRES III spectrograph at Bosscha Observatory. We processed the raw spectroscopy image to generate a high-resolution 1D spectrum. Employing advanced algorithms and calibration techniques, we extracted precise spectral information using our custom Python-based program. Our program offers a user-friendly interface and enhances accessibility for astronomers and researchers by allowing it to be accessed on a local server. We measured and determined the expansion velocity to be 193.637 km s⁻¹ from Hα observation — related to the circumstellar material responsible for the P Cygni profile and providing key information about stellar mass loss and evolutionary stage. Results were compared against IRAF-processed data, revealing valuable insights into the stellar characteristics.
